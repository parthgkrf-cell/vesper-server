Getting Started in 3 Steps

  1. Import Recipients: Prepare an Excel or CSV file. Ensure it contains the headers Name, Email, Affiliation, and Time. Drag it into the Import Excel workspace.
  2. Compose Your Template: Go to Compose Template and draft your message. Insert merge tags like {Name} or {Affiliation} to personalize the content. Look at the Personalized Live Preview on the right to make sure it looks perfect.
  3. Configure & Start: Select your sending method in Configuration (SMTP to send immediately, or IMAP to save drafts), configure your SMTP sender accounts, and click Start Scheduler in the sidebar.

Understanding Cloud Sending Modes

Cloud Background Send (SMTP)

Emails are dispatched automatically in the background using the SMTP protocol via the server. Once started, the scheduler processes your active queue 24/7 on the server even if you close this dashboard tab or shut down your device.

Cloud Background Draft (IMAP)

This mode connects to your mail account's IMAP server and saves the fully customized emails directly into your Drafts folder in the background. This allows you to inspect them manually before sending them out.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to keep the tab open?

No. The scheduler runs entirely in the server background. Once you click "Start Scheduler", the server handles the queue 24/7. You can close this browser tab, log out, or shut down your computer entirely, and emails will still be dispatched at their target times.

What happens if the scheduled time has already passed when I import?

The scheduler will treat it as "due immediately" and trigger/send it within the first few seconds of starting the scheduler engine.

Can I edit individual rows after importing?

You can manage the active status of scheduled items in the Schedule & Queue tab, check/uncheck items, trigger specific items immediately, or reset them.

Are my passwords and API keys secure?

Yes. Your configuration, keys, and credentials are saved securely in your unified config.json file on your deployed server. Vesper doesn't have access to your private database/server files, and data is only transmitted directly to your SMTP/IMAP servers.
